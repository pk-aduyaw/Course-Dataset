Price: The price of the car in US dollars. This is the target variable for the regression model.

Mileage: The total distance the car has traveled, measured in miles. Typically, higher mileage can correlate with lower prices.

Year: The manufacturing year of the car. More recent models usually have higher prices.

EngineSize: The size of the car's engine, measured in liters. Larger engines might indicate better performance but could also affect fuel efficiency.

Horsepower: The power output of the car's engine, measured in horsepower. Higher horsepower often correlates with better performance and higher prices.

Weight: The weight of the car, measured in pounds. Heavier cars might indicate larger, more luxurious models.

FuelEfficiency: The car's fuel efficiency, measured in miles per gallon (MPG). More fuel-efficient cars are often more economical and may be priced higher depending on market demand.

BrandScore: A score representing the popularity or reputation of the car's brand, on a scale from 0 to 10. A higher score usually correlates with higher brand desirability and price.
